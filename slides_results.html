Part II: Local Labor Market Heterogeneity

How Effects Vary by Labor Market Conditions

Theoretical Framework: SA and Labor Market Power

Key Mechanisms and Predictions

Monopsony & Labor Market Power

  • In concentrated markets, employers extract rents by paying wages below marginal productivity (Azar, Marinescu & Steinbaum, 2022)
  • SA may act as countervailing force—raising workers' outside option and improving bargaining position
  • Prediction: Larger income gains from SA in high-HHI markets where wage suppression is greatest

Reservation Wages & Job Search

  • SA raises reservation wages, allowing workers to be more selective about job quality
  • In tight labor markets, workers can leverage improved outside option to demand better wages/hours
  • Prediction: Hours and job quality effects strongest in low-unemployment settings

Liquidity vs. Moral Hazard

  • Chetty (2008): 60% of UI duration effects are liquidity, not moral hazard
  • In slack labor markets, liquidity relief may be most valuable—workers can wait for better matches
  • Prediction: Different channels dominate in different labor market conditions

Human Capital Investment

  • SA relaxes liquidity constraints that prevent education investment
  • Returns to human capital higher in competitive markets; opportunity cost lower in high unemployment
  • Prediction: Education enrollment increases most in competitive and high-unemployment LLMs

Key references: Azar et al. (2022, JHR); Chetty (2008, JPE); Manning (2003); Mortensen & Pissarides (1994)

LLM Heterogeneity: What We Learn

Synthesizing Evidence Across Labor Market Conditions

📊 SA as Countervailing Force Against Monopsony

  • Concentrated markets: Higher total income, gains at bottom of distribution
  • SA improves worker bargaining position where employers have most wage-setting power
  • Supports monopsony theory: transfers most beneficial where wage suppression is greatest

📚 SA Facilitates Human Capital Investment

  • Competitive markets + high unemployment: Increased HS enrollment
  • High unemployment lowers opportunity cost of education; competitive markets raise returns to skills
  • Consistent with liquidity constraint models—transfers unlock human capital accumulation

⚠️ Different Channels in Different Markets

  • High-HHI: SA enables accepting lower-wage, higher-hour jobs (compensating differentials)
  • Low unemployment: Workers leverage SA to negotiate better hours
  • High unemployment: Labor income declines suggest search/waiting effects

🎯 Policy Implications

  • Context matters: SA effects depend critically on local labor market structure
  • Targeting: Greatest income gains in concentrated markets—areas often overlooked
  • Long-run effects: Higher DI utilization in disadvantaged LLMs suggests potential scarring

Big Picture: Social assistance does not have uniform effects—its impacts depend on the degree of employer market power and local labor market conditions. These findings support models of monopsonistic wage-setting and suggest welfare policy should account for labor market structure.
