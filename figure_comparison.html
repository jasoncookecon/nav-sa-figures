Research Design

This study uses an examiner design exploiting random caseworker assignment within NAV offices to estimate causal effects of Social Assistance (SA) benefits on adult outcomes. The instrument is caseworker leniency (UJIVE - Uniformly Justified Leave-One-Out IV).

Sample: Norwegian-born adults (nor==1) aged 18-67 receiving Social Assistance during 2012-2022.

Distributional Outcomes

  • inc_tot_gt_p - Total Income: All income sources combined
  • inc_work_gt_p - Labor Income: Earnings from employment
  • taxable_income_gt_p - Taxable Income: Income subject to taxation

Each ridgeline plot shows effects across the income distribution. Lines represent percentile thresholds (p10-p90). Y-axis shows the probability of exceeding that percentile.

Split Levels

  • split0 - Reference group (e.g., Male, Low HHI, No shock)
  • split1 - Treatment group (e.g., Female, High HHI, Shock experienced)
  • pooled - Full sample (only in "all" split)

Full Sample (All)

Full sample results using the pooled Norwegian-born population. Shows effects of Amount, Duration, and Amount x Duration treatments.
